Plastic vs Aluminium

When discussing mechanical keyboards, we must first decide on the quality of build we want. Many in the enthusiast community prefers aluminium, but I would argue the choice is not so simple. There are factors beyond price that we should consider, when deciding between the two.

Plastic

Plastic keyboards, expectedly, are cheaper than aluminium boards. Consumerism tells us that aluminium is better, because its more expensive, but the choice is not so simple. Consider the following pros/cons:

Pros Cons
Cheaper Prone to scratches
Lighter Moves around on desk
Deeper sound signature Less sound variety

While we can continue listing more pros and cons (we haven't even touched hollowness), the idea is straightforward: plastic is not perfect, but it does come with its own set of benefits. Anyone who wishes for a deep sounding board that they can travel with should seriously consider a board made from plastic.

Aluminium

Aluminium boards come with a hefty price tag, and often times, they may not be worth it. But who am I to put a price tag on satisfaction? Expensive boards are often CNC-machined, are incredibly high quality, and built to last. These boards are the epitome of luxury typing.

Pros Cons
High quality build Expensive
Stable on desk Heavy and difficult to travel with
Large sound variety Default higher sound signature

Aluminium boards have a hefty price tag attached to them- but to some, they are worth every penny. Sleek build quality, combined with clean, consistent sound, is a dream for anyone who types for extended periods of time.
